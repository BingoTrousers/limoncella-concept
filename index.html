ore 19:10 · La Storia

Trecento gradini sopra il mare

Limoncella began in 1962 as a lemon stand on the mule path above Marina Grande — a plank table, a straw awning, Nonna Assunta pressing granita for the fishermen climbing home. The kitchen came later. The view was always here.

Four generations on, we still cook the way the coast taught us: whatever the boats bring in at dawn, whatever the garden gives by noon, finished with oil from our own gnarled trees and lemons picked an arm's reach from your table. Dinner begins when the sun starts its slow fall into the Tyrrhenian — and nobody has ever asked for the bill before dark.

Il mare insegna la pazienza; i limoni, la gioia.
— Assunta Ruocco, fondatrice
